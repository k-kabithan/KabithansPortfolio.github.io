Who am I?

Hi there! My name is Kabithan, and I am a recent First Class Honours graduate in Aeronautical Engineering. I have always been passionate about using my analytical and problem-solving skills to make a positive impact on the world, and I am particularly interested in using data and technology to address sustainability challenges.

During my studies, I focused on aircraft design and performance analysis, where I developed a strong understanding of mathematical modeling and data analysis. I have also completed many courseworks in data analysis and statistics, which allowed me to develop proficiency in a variety of software tools and programming languages, including Python and MATLAB.

As I transition into a career in data analysis, I am excited to apply my technical skills to real-world problems. I am highly motivated and organised, with excellent communication and teamwork abilities. I am always looking for ways to improve my skills and knowledge, and I am committed to using data to drive positive change in the world.

List of personal projects with a short description of the project.

Malaria Project:
Datasets collected by the World Health Organisation (WHO) were analysed using Python to identify correlations between the number of malaria cases and deaths, as well as other factors like region and year. The analysis showed that global malaria cases continue to rise due to insufficient funding and the effects of climate change. Despite this, fatalities fluctuated between 2000 and 2017, possibly due to difficulties in obtaining necessary medication. Certain regions and countries have higher rates of malaria.

    Exoplanets Project:
    The exoplanet dataset contains data on planets outside our solar system, including the year of discovery, detection method, mass and radius based on Jupiter and Earth, and other properties. The dataset was used to explore the distribution of exoplanets by detection method and year of discovery. Visualisations showed potential exoplanets for supporting life and the number discovered each year between 1992-2022. The insights gained can be used for further research in astronomy.

    COVID-19 Project:
    The COVID-19 dataset was used to explore the correlation between cases and deaths, vaccination efforts, and global impact. Insights were gained on death percentages by country, highest infection rates, and daily case and death counts. This analysis is important for understanding the impact of the pandemic and evaluating the effectiveness of interventions such as vaccinations.

    Nashville Housing Project:
    The housing dataset contains information on housing price, type of land, owner name and addresses, date sold and other information. This dataset was used to hone data cleaning skills such as converting data types, filling in null values, seperating property address column into individual columns such as city, state and address.
